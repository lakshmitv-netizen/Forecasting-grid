Key Differentiator Summary

Forecasting Grid occupies a unique position: It delivers enterprise-grade planning capabilities (value propagation, audit trails, hierarchical data management) with the ease-of-use of a spreadsheet. For Salesforce customers, it eliminates the integration complexity and context-switching required by standalone planning tools. The sweet spot is users who need more than Excel but less than Anaplan.

Conclusion

The Forecasting Grid prototype demonstrates strong fundamentals in visual design, interaction patterns, and core functionality. The "Delightful" and "Trust" categories score particularly well, indicating thoughtful attention to user experience polish and data integrity.

Key strengths: Research has been conducted to validate the problem space, AI capabilities have been planned, and accessibility requirements have been identified. These are now in implementation phase.

The primary gaps are in Efficiency (missing onboarding, help features) and Flexibility (localization, cross-device support). These are typical for a prototype-stage product and should be prioritized in the roadmap.

Recommendation: Complete the in-progress accessibility and AI implementations, then focus on onboarding as a critical priority before production release. The foundation is solid—completing the planned features will significantly elevate the overall UX score.
